Overview — what this CRM is

Flameback CRM runs the full client journey for the wealth-management business: from a fresh lead, through onboarding, to an actively managed client — with every team working off the same record.

The whole platform is organised around three things: Leads (the sales pipeline), Onboarding (turning an interested person into a signed, funded client), and Clients (the people we now manage money for). Around those sit the supporting teams — Compliance, Operations, Investment — and a cross-team Tasks & Requests system that lets anyone hand work to anyone with a full audit trail.

Use the “Viewing as” switcher (top-left) to see the CRM as each role. Each role only sees the menus relevant to their job. This Knowledge Hub is visible to everyone.

Who does what — duties by role

Relationship Manager (RM)

  • Owns the lead end-to-end: logs every call/meeting/WhatsApp, keeps the status and stage current.
  • Drives onboarding — clicks Start onboarding, fills the form with the client, picks the product fit, sends the agreement for e-sign.
  • Raises requests to Compliance / Operations / Investment when something is needed for their client.
  • Runs the client relationship after funding: review meetings, follow-ups, top-ups.

Qualifier (Lead Desk)

  • First touch for people who sign up in the app. Calls them, checks intent and fit.
  • Assigns the best-fit RM (by language / region / capacity / goal) and auto-creates the Google Meet invite.
  • Picks up drop-offs — if someone abandoned self-serve, the Qualifier routes them to an RM to finish.

Compliance

  • Verifies identity: Name vs PAN, PAN, DOB against uploaded documents.
  • Flags mismatches and raises them back to the RM; verifies when clean.
  • Owns KYC / CKYC / AML / PEP and re-KYC. A client cannot be fully onboarded until Compliance signs off.

Operations

  • Opens bank & demat (Zerodha) accounts, collects cheques, handles detail changes and login issues.
  • Reconciles suspense holdings and corporate actions with the custodian.

Investment

  • Builds and maintains each client's IPS (Investment Policy Statement) and target allocation.
  • Runs quarterly portfolio reviews and annual IPS reviews; rebalances when drift exceeds target.
  • Handles strategy changes and suspense reconciliation on the investment side.

Leadership / Dept Heads

  • See team throughput and a live “Needs your attention” list per department.
  • Intervene directly — reassign, escalate, write off a stuck lead, mark a review handled, nudge a team member. Every action is logged.

How a lead flows

A lead moves through four stages. The RM sets the stage; the CRM tracks the rest.

StageMeaning
L1 — ColdInitial outreach. The Five-Strike Rule applies here.
L2 — Hot / HoldingHas shown interest or is in active consideration.
L3 — OnboardingProceeded to onboarding — KYC / paperwork in progress.
L4 — Pre-InvestmentOnboarding done, first trades not yet deployed.

Flow: Lead captured → worked through L1–L4 → Onboarding → Funded client. Leads that go nowhere move to the Out List (with a reason) and can be revived later.

The Five-Strike Rule

At L1 only, if a lead has had 5 outreach attempts (calls / messages) with no response or interest, the CRM flags it for review. The RM either records that the lead responded (keep) or moves it to the Out List.

Leadership can see all five-strike leads in the Head of Sales view and write them off in one click.

App sign-ups & the Assignment Queue

When someone signs up in the app they choose one of two paths:

  • Self-serve — they proceed on their own.
  • RM-assisted (“talk to us”) — they want an advisor to walk them through.

They can switch paths any time, and a self-serve drop-off becomes a recovery lead. New app sign-ups land in the Assignment Queue (Qualifier role):

1
Qualifier opens the lead and reviews captured details & intent.
2
Picks the best-fit RM (a recommendation is shown with the reason).
3
Confirms — the RM is assigned and a Google Meet invite is auto-created with the client, RM and Qualifier.

Onboarding & the form

The RM-assisted path is driven from the Onboarding menu. Each lead row has a Start onboarding button (or Resume · X% if a form already exists).

1
Click Start onboarding — it asks for the client's mobile number first.
2
Every form is linked by mobile number. If the client already started (self-serve, or with another RM) the same form resumes with everything pre-filled. For a known app lead the number auto-fills.
3
Fill the long form — personal, contact, financial, objective, advisory routing (product fit), risk profile, bank/demat, nominee, declarations. Progress shows live at the top.
4
Advisory routing: based on the conversation the RM selects the product — PMS Lite (RIA), PMS, or International investing — with a short rationale. If the client already chose in-app, it's pre-filled.
5
At the end, send the e-sign request for the agreement (enabled once the form is mostly complete). That advances the client toward signed & funded.
Because forms are keyed by mobile, a dropped-off client never loses progress — whoever picks them up resumes the exact same form.

Active clients

Once funded, a person becomes a Client with a unique Client ID. The profile has tabs: Overview, Investment & SIP, Risk Profile & Agreement, Strategies, KYC, Portfolio (synced from FBC-Admin), Invoices and Call Sync. An onboarding stepper shows where they are (Sign-up → Questionnaire → Agreement → KYC → Invested).

Tasks & handoffs — how to assign work

Anything one team needs from another goes through Tasks & Requests. This is the single place handoffs happen, with a full activity log.

Raising a request

1
Open Tasks & Requests → + New request (or raise one from a client).
2
Pick the team, the task type, and optionally a specific person. Set priority, due date, link the client, add notes.
3
It lands in that team's inbox. Use “Assigned to me” to see what's yours.

Working a ticket

  • The board has four columns — Open · In Progress · Blocked · Done. Drag a card between columns to change its status.
  • Post updates in the Activity log; status moves and reassignments are recorded there automatically.
  • Reassigning requires a reason — changing the team or person reveals a required reason field, logged as part of the history.

Compliance checks

In the Verification view, Compliance compares each client's entered details against uploaded documents — Name (as per PAN), PAN, DOB — plus agreement, questionnaire, KRA KYC and CKYC.

  • If something doesn't match, ⚠ Raise to RM sends it back to be corrected (a task is created).
  • When everything is clean, Mark verified. A client's compliance status is one of Verified · Pending · Flagged.

Reviews & reminders

Every funded client has two cadences: an annual IPS review and a quarterly portfolio review. The Reviews & Reminders view shows who is due this week so nothing slips. Reminders/emails go out from the RM's own Gmail via the composer.

Leadership view

The Leadership role has one screen with a department toggle (Sales / Investment / Compliance / Operations). For each department it shows:

  • Team throughput — how much work got done (tickets closed, outreach, meetings, reviews).
  • ⚠ Needs your attention — the escalation list: five-strike leads, overdue reviews, flagged clients, blocked tickets.
  • Scorecard — output per person, to spot who is overloaded or stalled.

Heads can act right there: reassign, escalate, write off, mark a review done, or nudge a team member — all logged.
